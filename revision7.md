**B. The Cantonese/Punti (本地人) (Textbook P.210, 218)**

1. A clan (族) is a large group of **families** with the **same** ancestor (祖先) and surname.

2. The Cantonese/Punti had about 40 clans. Among them, the Tangs (鄧族), the Haus (侯族), the Pangs (彭族), the Lius (廖族) and the Mans (文族) were the most powerful. They were known as the **Five Great Clans of the New Territories** (新界五大族).

3. Among the major dialect groups, the Cantonese/Punti (本地人) were wealthier and most powerful because:

(a) Among the major dialect groups, the Cantonese/Punti were the **earliest** group to settle in Hong Kong. Therefore, they could settle in the **fertile land** in the New Territories, which were suitable for **farming lands**.

(b) After they settled down, they developed more and more **influence**. They owned lots of land and became rich landlords (地主). They also owned many **markets** in the New Territories. As a result, they had great **economic** power.

(c) They were very **concerned** about **education**. They used ancestral halls (祠堂) and study halls (書室) as **schools**. As a result, members of the Tangs (鄧族) and the Haus (侯族) got scholarly titles (功名) through examination. Then, they could become **officials** (官員). This enhanced the **power** and **influence** (影響力) of the Cantonese/Punti.

**C. Houses and buildings (Textbook P.212–216)**  

The Cantonese/Punti and the Hakka built villages on **land** while the boat people and the Fuklo lived on **boats**.  

**1. Cantonese/Punti and Hakka**  

(a) **Walled villages (圍村)**  
- As there were many pirates (海盜) and bandits (土匪) during the Ming and Qing Dynasties, villagers built **walls** and dug **moats** (護城河) around their villages to protect themselves. These villages were called **walled villages**.  
- Today, there are about 70 walled villages in the New Territories. Most of them are situated in **Yuen Long**, Kam Tin, Sheung Shui and Fanling.  
- Walled villages in the New Territories were divided into ‘Punti walled villages’ and ‘Hakka walled villages’.  
  - **Punti** walled villages were mainly built by the **Five Great Clans**. These villages were **well-planned**.  
  - Compared with Punti walled villages, **Hakka** walled villages were **smaller** in scale.  

(b) **Ancestral halls (祠堂)**  
- **Ancestral halls** were the most important buildings in villages.  
  **Functions (功能):**  
  - Villagers **worshiped** their ancestors (拜祖先) in the ancestral halls. They housed the soul tablets (神主牌) of the villagers’ ancestors.  
  - Village chief (村長) and male elders went there to discuss village **affairs**.  
  - Village chief and male elders also heard **cases** (審案) there.  
  - They held **celebrations** there.  
  - Ancestral halls were also used as **schools**.

**2. Boat people and Fuklo**  
(a) The boat people and the Fuklo lived on **boats**.  
(b) Some of them chose to live near the **coast** (沿岸) later on. They used wood, grasses and iron sheets to build **stilt houses** (棚屋). The largest number of stilt houses are gathered in Tai O.

**D. Beliefs and customs (Textbook P.219–220)**  
1. The four major dialect groups kept many traditional **Chinese** beliefs (信仰) and customs (習俗). However, their different backgrounds (背景) and **lifestyles** (生活方式) led to the rise of the multi‑cultural tradition (多元文化傳統) of Hong Kong.  

2. **Ancestral worship (拜祖先)**  
The early residents in Hong Kong kept **this Chinese tradition** (傳統) of ancestral worshipping and held ceremonies (儀式) to worship their ancestors regularly.  

3. **Popular religion (民間宗教)**  
(a) The early residents in Hong Kong followed **Chinese** popular religion, such as the worship of Kwan Tai (關帝).  
(b) The boat people and the Fuklo mainly worshipped **sea** gods such as Tin Hau (天后) and Hung Shing (洪聖) for a safe return from sea.  
(c) The Cantonese and the Hakka also worshipped Tin Hau.  


4. Festivals  
(a) The early residents in Hong Kong celebrated traditional Chinese festivals.

(b) For example, they celebrated the Chinese New Year, the Mid‑Autumn Festival and the Winter Solstice Festival (冬至) for family reunion (團聚). They held the Ching Ming Festival and the Chung Yeung Festival for ancestral worship.
