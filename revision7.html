Dialect Groups

The Four Major Dialect Groups

Between the 10th and 17th centuries, four major groups from Mainland China settled in Hong Kong. Each had its own dialect, origin, and way of life.

Group Arrived Dialect Origin Settlement in HK
Cantonese / Punti (本地人) 10th c. Wai Tau (圍頭話) Guangdong (廣東) Fertile plains in northern New Territories
Hakka (客家人) 17th c. Hakka Guangdong & Fujian Distant, infertile regions in NT
Fuklo (福佬人) 11th c. Minnan (閩南話) Fujian & east Guangdong Eastern waters of HK — most lived on boats
Boat People (水上人) Unclear Cantonese & Minnan Coast of Fujian & Guangdong Coast of NT & HK Island — most lived on boats
📝 Exam Note: All four groups came from Mainland China (mainly Guangdong and Fujian). Their daily lives shared much with people in the Mainland. HK has been part of China's territory since the Qin Dynasty (214 BC).
Livelihoods

How Each Group Made a Living

The four dialect groups developed different livelihoods based on where they settled.

Group Settlement & Land Livelihood (謀生)
Cantonese / Punti Fertile plains in northern NT (1) Farmed
(2) Set up markets/bazaars (市集) to sell farm products
Hakka Distant, infertile regions in NT (1) Farmed (2) Salt making (3) Brick making (4) Quarrying (採石) (5) Stone cutting (6) Set up markets
Fuklo Eastern waters — most lived on boats (1) Oyster farming (2) Pearl diving (3) Shipping goods (航運)
Boat People Coast of NT & HK Island — most lived on boats (1) Oyster farming (2) Pearl diving (3) Shipping goods

🌾 Land-based groups

Cantonese/Punti and Hakka lived on land and relied on farming and related industries. The Cantonese had the best land; the Hakka made do with less fertile areas through diverse occupations.

🌊 Sea-based groups

Fuklo and Boat people lived mainly on boats and relied on the sea for their livelihoods: oyster farming, pearl diving, and shipping.

Power & Influence

The Cantonese/Punti & the Five Great Clans

Among the four groups, the Cantonese/Punti were the wealthiest and most powerful. Here's why.

Five Great Clans

🏛️ 新界五大族

A clan (族) is a large group of families with the same ancestor and surname. The Cantonese/Punti had about 40 clans, but five were the most powerful: Tang (鄧), Hau (侯), Pang (彭), Liu (廖), and Man (文).

Why were they powerful?

💪 Three Reasons for Cantonese/Punti Power

  • Earliest arrival: They were the first group to settle in HK (10th c.), so they claimed the fertile land in the New Territories, ideal for farming.
  • Economic power: They owned lots of land (became rich landlords) and controlled many markets (bazaars) in the New Territories, giving them great economic power.
  • Education → Officials: They used ancestral halls and study halls as schools. Members passed the civil service examination and became officials (官員), enhancing their power and influence.
📝 Exam Question: "Why were the Cantonese/Punti wealthier and more powerful than other dialect groups?" Answer with the three reasons above: earliest arrival → fertile land, economic power (land + markets), education → scholarly titles → officials.
Architecture

Houses & Buildings of Early Hong Kong

Land-based groups (Punti, Hakka) built walled villages; sea-based groups (Fuklo, Boat people) lived on boats or in stilt houses.

Walled Villages (圍村)

🏘️ Punti vs Hakka

During the Ming and Qing dynasties, pirates and bandits threatened villages. Villagers built walls and dug moats (護城河) for protection. About 70 walled villages remain today, mostly in Yuen Long, Kam Tin, Sheung Shui, and Fanling.

  • Punti walled villages: Built by the Five Great Clans — well-planned, larger scale
  • Hakka walled villages: Smaller in scale compared to Punti villages
Ancestral Halls (祠堂)

🏛️ Functions

  • Worship ancestors — housed soul tablets
  • Village chief & male elders discussed village affairs
  • Heard cases (審案)
  • Held celebrations
  • Used as schools
Study Halls (書室)

📚 Functions

  • Used as schools for children
  • Some also used for ancestral worship
Boat People & Fuklo

🚤 Stilt Houses (棚屋)

The boat people and Fuklo lived on boats. Later, some moved near the coast and built stilt houses using wood, grasses, and iron sheets. The largest concentration of stilt houses is in Tai O.

Culture

Beliefs, Customs & Festivals

The four groups kept traditional Chinese beliefs, but their different backgrounds and lifestyles created Hong Kong's multicultural tradition.

Ancestral Worship

🙏 拜祖先

All early residents kept this Chinese tradition. They held regular ceremonies to worship their ancestors.

Popular Religion

🏯 民間宗教

  • All groups followed Chinese popular religion (e.g. Kwan Tai / 關帝)
  • Boat people & Fuklo mainly worshipped sea gods: Tin Hau (天后) and Hung Shing (洪聖) for safe sea journeys
  • Cantonese & Hakka also worshipped Tin Hau
Festivals

🎉 節日

  • Family reunion: Chinese New Year, Mid-Autumn Festival, Winter Solstice (冬至)
  • Ancestral worship: Ching Ming Festival, Chung Yeung Festival

🌏 Multi-Cultural Tradition

The four groups all kept traditional Chinese beliefs and customs, but their different backgrounds and lifestyles meant that Hong Kong developed a multi-cultural tradition (多元文化傳統).

📝 Exam Note: Boat people and Fuklo worshipped sea gods (Tin Hau, Hung Shing) because their lives depended on the sea. This is a common link question!
Conclusion

Cultural Relations: Hong Kong & Mainland China

How similar was Hong Kong's traditional culture to that of Mainland China? Evidence from three areas.

AreaCultural RelationEvidence
🏘️ Architecture Similar Walled villages with walls, moats, and watchtowers can be found in both Hong Kong and Guangdong.
📚 Education Similar Boys in both HK and China were taught Chinese classics, history, Confucianism (儒家思想), and calligraphy (書法). Both hoped children would pass the civil service examination and become officials.
🙏 Beliefs & Customs Similar Both worshipped their ancestors. Both shared similar festivals (e.g. Chinese New Year, Mid-Autumn Festival).

📝 Conclusion

As Hong Kong shared similar traditional architecture, education, beliefs, and customs with Mainland China, the cultural relations between Hong Kong and the Mainland were very close.

📝 DBQ Strategy: This is a typical Data-Based Question (DBQ) — you are given evidence and asked to draw a conclusion. Always state the relation (similar), then give specific evidence from each area, then state the conclusion (close cultural relations).
