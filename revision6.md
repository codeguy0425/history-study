### ⚖️ Political Evolution

#### 1. Roman Monarchy
- Rome was originally ruled by **kings** under a **monarchy**.

#### 2. Roman Republic
- Romans **overthrew a cruel king** and established a **republic** (democracy-like system, similar to Athens).  

Political system of ancient rome changed from a monarchy to a republic.

- A **republic** = a state ruled by the **people**.  



- Male citizens elected rulers.

---



### 🏛 Structure of the Roman Republic
the government was divided into two branches:

- **Executive Branch:**  
  - Two **consuls** (highest officials) elected by male citizens.  
  - **Senate** advised the consuls.  

- **Legislative Branch:**  
  - **Legislative assemblies** discussed and passed laws.  



👉 Power was **separated** among **consuls, Senate, and legislative assemblies**, would not be held by a single person.

### ⚔️ Power Struggles
1. **Julius Caesar**  (the general) 
   - Defeated the senators and became **dictator for life**. (held all the power, power began to be held by one person)  
   - he made the roman republic become more powerful and people's lives improved.
   therefore he got the support from soldiers and common people.
   
   Later assassinated by senators.  

2. **Octavian Caesar (adopted son) & Antony (general)**  
   - Cooperated to defeat the senators. they ruled the republic together.  
   - Eventually fought a **civil war**, where Octavian defeated Antony.  

---

### 👑 Roman Empire
- After victory, **Octavian Caesar** restored **monarchy** and founded the **Roman Empire**.  
- He became the **first emperor**.  
- In the empire, emperors held **all power**; citizens no longer elected leaders.  
- The **executive and legislative branches lost authority**.

---

### 🌟 Golden Age
- The Roman Empire represented the **golden age of ancient Rome** — powerful and expansive.  
- Its territory covered ** Europe, Asia, and Africa**.  
- The **Mediterranean Sea** became the Empire’s “inner lake,” surrounded entirely by Roman lands.  

---

### 📉 Decline and Division
- In **AD 395**, the Empire was divided into:  
  - **Western Roman Empire**  
  - **Eastern Roman Empire**  

- **Western Roman Empire:**  
  - Fell in **AD 476** to the **Germanic peoples** from the north.  
  - Marked the **end of ancient times** and the **start of medieval Europe**.  

- **Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine):**  
  - Fell in **AD 1453** to the **Ottoman Turks**.  
  - Marked the **end of medieval times** and the **start of modern Europe**.

### 🎭 Greek Myths
- Ancient Greeks worshipped many **gods and goddesses**.  
- **Zeus** was the head of the gods.  
- Gods were imagined as **half‑god, half‑man**, with **human feelings** but also great **strength** and **wisdom**.  
- These myths provided **themes for European literature and drama**.

---

### 🏟 Olympic Games
- Greeks believed some gods lived on **Mount Olympus**.  
- The **Olympic Games** were held to honour **Zeus**.  
- During the games, city‑states **stopped fighting** so athletes could compete safely.  
- The **Modern Olympic Games** continue this tradition, held every **four years** in different countries.

### 🗣 Influence on Languages
- Greek myths shaped **European languages**.  
- Many modern English words come from Greek origins.  
- Example: **“Music”** comes from the Greek word **Muses**, goddesses of the arts.

---

### 📚 Philosophy
- The word **“philosophy”** means “love of wisdom” in Greek.  
- **Socrates, Plato, Aristotle** → three Greek sages, regarded as the **founders of Western philosophy**.  
- Their ideas became the foundation of European intellectual traditions.

---

### 🎨 Sculpture
- **Ancient Greece:**  
  - Used **bronze** and **marble** for statues.  
  - Themes: worship, myths, athletes.  
  - Focused on **ideal human proportions** of huamn body, lifelike personality, and body movement.  
  - Deeply influenced later Western sculpture.  

- **Ancient Rome:**  
  - Focused on **realistic images** of mortals (emperors, nobles).  
  - Paid close attention to **details**.  
  - Greatly impacted the development of Western art.

### 🏛 Ancient Greece
- Important buildings (especially **temples**) were surrounded by tall stone **columns**.

public participation and secret ballot.

### 🏟 Ancient Rome
- **Republican system:**  
  - A republic = a state ruled by the **people**.  
  - Spread from Rome to Europe and still influences modern countries.  

- **Centralization:**  
  - Roman imperial system emphasized **administrative centralization**.  
  - In modern states, the **central government** holds supreme power, while **local governments** derive authority from it.

### ⚖️ Ancient Greece
- Athenians valued **civil rights** and **law**.  
- Romans later learned the idea of the **rule of law** from Greece.  
- Many modern countries adopted the **jury system**, making justice more open and fair.

---

### 🏛 Ancient Rome
- Romans believed **all citizens were equal before the law**.  
- Citizens had rights to a **fair trial** and to **appeal**.  
- These principles remain central to modern legal systems today.
