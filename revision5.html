Prehistoric Times (史前時代)

The period before writing was invented. No written records exist. Humans used stone tools, lived as hunter-gatherers, and later developed early agriculture.

📝 Exam Focus: Prehistoric = NO writing. The invention of writing (~4000 BC) marks the shift to Historic times.
Key Date

4000 BC — Writing Invented

Writing systems first developed in the Fertile Crescent (Mesopotamia) and Nile Valley (Egypt). This marks the beginning of historic / ancient times.

Key Date

AD 1453 — Fall of Constantinople

The Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire fell to the Ottoman Turks. This event marks the end of the Medieval period and the start of Modern times.

Origins

Rise of Classical Civilization (古典文明)

Greco-Roman civilization did not appear in isolation. It was shaped by earlier cultures and external influences from Asia and Africa.

External Influence

🌍 Fertile Crescent & Nile Valley

Early European civilizations learned agriculture, metalworking, and writing from the Fertile Crescent (Asia) and the Nile Valley (Africa) because Europe was geographically close to these regions.

🏺 Minoan Civilization (米諾斯)

Began around 3000 BC on the island of Crete in the Mediterranean Sea. Founded by the Minoans, a seafaring people.

⚔️ Mycenaean Civilization (邁錫尼)

The Mycenaeans from the Greek peninsula conquered Crete, learned from the Minoans, and developed their own civilization on the mainland.

🏛️ Classical Civilization

In the 8th century BC, the ancient Greeks entered their Golden Age. The Romans later learned from the Greeks. Together they formed Greco-Roman (Classical) civilization — the foundation of European culture.

Causation

Geography & Formation of City-States

This cause-effect relationship is frequently tested in S1 exams. Learn the logical chain.

Geographical Cause

⛰️ Mountainous Terrain

Greece's landscape was dominated by mountains and deep valleys. Travel and communication by land were extremely difficult, isolating communities from one another.

→
Political Effect

🏛️ Independent City-States

Instead of unifying under one ruler, each community developed its own independent government, forming city-states (polis) — small, sovereign states with their own laws and armies.

🌟 Two Key Features of Greek Politics

  • Co-existence: Many city-states lived side by side, each independent and self-governing.
  • Diverse governance: Different city-states had different political systems — monarchy, oligarchy, or democracy.
Comparison

👑 Monarchy (君主制)

Who rules? A single hereditary ruler — a king or queen.

Example: The Kingdom of Macedonia in northern Greece.

💡 Exam Note: Macedonian kings held absolute power, unlike the citizens of democratic Athens who voted on issues collectively.

⚠️ Limits of Athenian Democracy

Athenian democracy was not like modern democracy. Only free adult male citizens had political rights. Slaves, women, children, and resident foreigners were completely excluded from political participation.

Hellenistic Legacy

Alexander the Great & Spread of Greek Culture

Greek civilization spread far beyond Greece through the conquests of Alexander the Great.

King of Macedonia

👑 Alexander the Great

Alexander (4th century BC) built a vast empire covering parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa — Greece, Egypt, Persia, and as far as India.

Result

🌍 Spread of Greek Civilization

As a result of Alexander's conquests, Greek culture, language, art, and science spread widely across all three continents, blending with local cultures to create Hellenistic civilization.

📝 Map Reference: See textbook p.87 for Alexander's empire map. You may be asked to identify the territories he conquered.

🏛️ Macedonia

Macedonia was a monarchy in northern Greece. King Philip II first united the Greek city-states under Macedonian rule. His son Alexander continued the conquests on a much larger scale.

🏙️ Alexandria

Alexander founded many cities named Alexandria (most famously in Egypt), which became major centres of Greek learning, trade, and culture.
