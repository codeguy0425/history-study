**A. Europe entered Medieval Times (Textbook P.116)**  

(prehistoric times → historic times)  
ancient times → medieval times → modern times  
4000 BC → AD 476 → AD 1453  

**Medieval times of Europe**

1. From the timeline above, the medieval times of Europe started in AD **476** and ended in AD **1453**. It lasted for about **1000** years.  

2. In AD 476, the fall of the **Western** Roman Empire marked the beginning of the medieval times of Europe.  

3. In AD 1453, the fall of the **Eastern** Roman Empire marked the end of the medieval times of Europe.  

4. During the early medieval times, **wars** were frequent (頻繁) in Western Europe.  

5. The **classical** civilization (古典文明) declined (衰落).  

6. Two important developments:  
- **feudal system** (封建制度) was adopted (採用).  
- **Christendom** (基督教世界) gradually formed.  

---

**B. Characteristics / features of the feudal society (封建社會) (Textbook P.118–122)**  

(1) **Rigid class structure (嚴格的階級結構) / Low social mobility (社會流動性低)**  
- The medieval European society was organised into a hierarchy (等級制度).  
- The four classes from top to bottom were  
  (1) *king*  
  (2) *upper noble*  
  (3) *lower noble* or *knight (騎士)*  
  (4) *peasants*  
- It was difficult for people to change their social classes.  

(2) **Hereditary system (世襲制度)**  
- People passed their *positions*, *title*, and *lands* to their *heirs (繼承人)*.  

(3) **Contractual relationship (合約關係)**  
- In a feudal society, an *overlord (領主)* and his *vassals (附庸)* had a contractual relationship.  

**Who is overlord? Who is vassal?**  

Diagram:  
- King → (Give land to upper nobles) → Upper nobles  
  (King is overlord; Upper nobles are vassals, loyal to the king)  
- Upper nobles → (Give land to lower nobles) → Lower nobles (Knights)  
  (Upper nobles are overlords; Lower nobles are vassals, loyal to upper nobles)  
- Lower nobles → (Allow peasants to farm on their lands) → Peasants  
  (Lower nobles are overlords; Peasants are vassals, work for the overlords)

(4) **Manors (莊園)**  
- The overlords set up manors.  
- The overlords lived in the **castles** (堡壘) in the manors.  
- **knight** and **peasants** also lived in the manors.  

(5) **Strong nobles**  
- Nobles had **strong** power.  
- The king was the ruler **in name** (名義上) only. The king could not exercise (行使) power on his vassal’s land.  

(6) **Knights and chivalry (騎士精神)**  
- Knights were professional **cavalry** (專業騎兵).  
- The king and upper nobles granted them **knighthood** (騎士身份) and **lands**.  
- Knights had to follow the **rules of chivalry** (騎士精神).  

➢ loyalty (忠心)  
➢ bravery (勇敢)  
➢ diligence (勤奮)  
➢ protecting the weak and  
➢ selfless sacrifice (無私犧牲)

**C. Legacy (遺產) and significance (重要性) of medieval European civilization (Textbook P.125–133)**  

(1) **Contractual spirit (合約精神) and constitution (憲法)**  
- Both overlords and vassals valued (重視) their feudal rights (權利) and duties (責任) in their contractual relationship.  
- The contractual spirit (合約精神) became a social norm (社會規範).  
- Magna Carta (大憲章) of England and the Constitution (憲法) of the United States were based on the contractual spirit.  

(2) **Rise of towns (城鎮興起)**  
- Later, some craftsmen (工匠) and merchants (商人) started to live outside manors.  
  Their living and working areas developed into towns.  
  Old towns revived (復興) and new towns developed.  
- Life in towns freed Europeans from the feudal society.  
  ➤ Overlords’ control of towns became weak.  
  ➤ Craftsmen and merchants became influential (有影響力) in towns.  
  ➤ More and more people were attracted to live in towns. As a result, urbanization (城市化) started.

(3) **Rise of universities**  
- Townsmen pursued (追求) knowledge other than religious aspect.  
- They started to study in some church schools. This gave rise to many universities in Europe.  
- Besides religious knowledge (宗教知識), these universities also taught medicine, law and philosophy (哲學), and so on.  
- Present‑day degree system (大學學位制度) (bachelor 學士; master 碩士; doctor 博士) and academic dress (學袍) are important traditions (傳統) inherited (傳承) from Medieval Times.

**D. The making of Christendom (基督教世界) (Textbook P.134–142)**  

**(1) Development of Christianity (基督教) in the Roman Empire (Ancient Times)**  
- Christians (基督徒) were *persecuted* (迫害) by the Roman government as they did not worship Roman gods and the emperors (皇帝).  
- Later, a Roman emperor stopped persecuting Christians and gave people *religious freedom* (宗教自由).  
- In AD 380, Christianity became the *official* religion (官方宗教) of the Roman Empire.  
- The number of Christians *increased*.  

**(2) Development of Christianity in Medieval Times**  
(a) *Split (分裂)* in Christianity  
- When the Roman Empire was *split* into the *Eastern* and *Western* Roman Empire, the Church (教會) also split into:  
  → the *Eastern Orthodox* Church (東正教會) and  
  → the *Roman Catholic* Church (羅馬天主教會)  

(b) *Formation (形成) of Christendom (基督教世界)*  
- Although the Church was split, Christianity continued to *develop* in Europe.  
- With the spread of Christianity, *Christendom* appeared in Europe.  
- The influence (影響力) of the *Roman Catholic* Church in *Western Europe* increased continuously.

**(c) Influence (影響力) of the Church**  
- The Church had a very **high** position (地位) among Europeans and influenced their **every** aspect of life.  
- The **Pope (教皇)** was the leader of the Church.  
  ➤ Everyone, including the **king**, had to obey him.  
  ➤ The Pope could **excommunicate (驅逐出教會)** the kings and **nobles** if they disobeyed his orders.  
- The Church was **wealthy** as it owned a lot of **lands** and collected **taxes**. Rich **nobles** left their possessions (財產) to the Church.  

**(d) Functions (功能) of the Church**  
- **Political function:**  
  The Church had great **political power** (政治權力).  
  ➤ The king had to be **crowned (加冕)** by the Pope.  
  ➤ The Pope used his power to **settle disputes (解決爭執)** and **mediate wars (調停戰爭)**.  
  This helped **maintain (維持) political stability (穩定)** in Europe.  

- **Legal (司法) function:**  
  ➤ The Church made **laws** and set up **courts**.  
  ➤ These helped **maintain law and order**.  

---


**Social function:**  
- The Church provided many *social services* (社會服務) such as looking after the elderly and children, treating patients and building roads and bridges.  

**Educational function:**  
- The Church set up a lot of *church schools*.  
- They taught *Latin* and *religious knowledge*.  

**Cultural function:**  
- The Church preserved (保存) *classical* civilization.  
- The *monks* (教士) *copied ancient texts* (文章) and classics (經典著作) in monasteries (修道院).
