### 🏺 Key Points
Greco-Roman / classic civilization were the earliest civilizations in Europe and the origin of the European civilization,
civilization had already begun to develop in Greece.

#### 1. Early European Civilizations
- **Influences:** From the **Fertile Crescent** (Asia) and **Nile Valley** (Africa) due to Europe was close to these places.  
- **Minoan Civilization:** Began around **3000 BC** on **Crete** island in the Mediterranean sea; founded by the **Minoans**.  
- **Mycenaean Civilization:** The **Mycenaeans** from the Greek peninsula conquered Crete, learned from the Minoans, and developed their own civilization.

#### 2. Birth of Classical Civilization
- In the **8th century BC**, based on the Minoan civilization and the Mycenaean civilization mentioned above, the **ancient Greeks** entered their **Golden Age**.  
- The **Romans** later learned from the Greeks.  
- Together, the **Greek** and **Roman** civilizations formed the **Greco‑Roman (Classical)** civilization — the foundation of European culture.

### 🏛 Key Ideas

#### 1. Formation of City‑States
- **Main cause:** **Geographical barriers** — Greece’s mountainous terrain made travel and communication difficult.  
- **Effect:** Cities became **isolated** and developed **independent governments**, forming **city‑states** (polis).  

#### 2. Political Development
- Two major features:  
  - **Co‑existence** of multiple city‑states.  
  - **Diverse forms of governance**, meaning each city‑state had its own political system (e.g., monarchy, oligarchy, democracy).

### 🏛 Overview of City‑States
- Ancient Greece had **many independent city‑states** of varying sizes.  
- Each city‑state included a **city and its surrounding area**, governed independently.  
- The two most representative were **Athens** and **Sparta**.

### ⚖️ Forms of Governance
Ancient Greek city‑states had **three main forms of government**:
- **Monarchy** → ruled by kings (e.g., **Macedonia**)  
- **Oligarchy** → ruled by a small nobles (e.g., **Sparta**)  
- **Democracy** → ruled by the people (e.g., **Athens**)  

👉 Athens allowed **more political participation** compared to Sparta and Macedonia.

---

### 👥 Who Could Participate in politics in ancient Greece?
- Only **free adult male citizens** had political rights.  
- **Slaves, children, and women** did not have this right.
