Chronology

Europe Enters Medieval Times

Medieval times lasted about 1000 years — from the fall of the Western Roman Empire to the fall of the Eastern Roman Empire.

Start

AD 476 — Fall of Western Roman Empire

The Western Roman Empire fell to Germanic peoples. This marked the beginning of the Medieval period in Europe.

End

AD 1453 — Fall of Eastern Roman Empire

The Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire fell to the Ottoman Turks. This marked the end of the Medieval period.

📅 Key Features of Early Medieval Times

  • Wars were frequent in Western Europe
  • Classical civilization (Greco-Roman) declined
  • The feudal system (封建制度) was adopted
  • Christendom (基督教世界) gradually formed

Feudal Society

Rigid Class Structure & Hereditary System

Medieval European society was organised into a strict hierarchy with very low social mobility.

👑 King
Ruler in name — often weak in practice
▼
🏛️ Upper Nobles
Dukes, earls — owned large estates
▼
⚔️ Lower Nobles / Knights (騎士)
Professional cavalry — granted knighthood
▼
🌾 Peasants / Serfs (農奴)
Worked the land — lowest class
Low Social Mobility

🔒 Rigid Class Structure

It was very difficult for people to change their social class. You were born into a class and usually stayed there for life.

Inherited

📜 Hereditary System (世襲制度)

People passed their positions, titles, and lands to their heirs (繼承人). A noble's son became a noble; a peasant's child remained a peasant.

Feudal System

Overlord-Vassal Contractual Relationships

In feudal society, every level of the hierarchy was linked by a contract — land in exchange for loyalty and service.

The Feudal Contract

🤝 How It Worked

An overlord (領主) granted land (fief) to his vassal (附庸). In return, the vassal promised loyalty, military service, and taxes.

Overlord→ Gave →VassalDuty of Vassal
👑 KinglandUpper NoblesLoyalty to the king, military service
🏛️ Upper NobleslandLower Nobles (Knights)Protection, military service
⚔️ Lower Noblesland to farmPeasants / SerfsWork the land, pay taxes, provide food

🏰 Manors (莊園)

Overlords set up manors where they lived in castles (堡壘). Knights and peasants also lived in the manor. The manor was a self-sufficient community.

👑 Strong Nobles, Weak King

Nobles had strong power. The king was the ruler in name (名義上) only. He could not exercise power on his vassal's land without permission.

Knights

Knights & the Code of Chivalry

Knights were the professional cavalry of medieval Europe, bound by a strict code of honour.

Who were knights?

⚔️ Professional Cavalry

  • Knights were professional cavalry (專業騎兵)
  • The king and upper nobles granted them knighthood (騎士身份) and lands
  • They formed the military backbone of feudal society
Chivalry

🛡️ Rules of Chivalry (騎士精神)

  • Loyalty (忠心) — to overlord and king
  • Bravery (勇敢) — in battle
  • Diligence (勤奮) — in duty
  • Protecting the weak
  • Selfless sacrifice (無私犧牲)

Legacy

Legacy & Significance of Medieval Civilization

The Middle Ages left lasting contributions to modern political, urban, and educational systems.

Contractual Spirit

📜 合約精神 & Constitution

Both overlords and vassals valued their feudal rights and duties. The contractual spirit (合約精神) became a social norm. Magna Carta (大憲章) of England and the US Constitution were based on this principle.

Urbanization

🏙️ Rise of Towns (城鎮興起)

Craftsmen and merchants started living outside manors. Their areas developed into towns. Overlords' control weakened. Urbanization (城市化) began, attracting more people.

Education

🎓 Rise of Universities

Townsmen pursued knowledge beyond religion. Church schools evolved into universities teaching medicine, law, and philosophy. Present-day degree system (bachelor, master, doctor) and academic dress (學袍) are traditions inherited from Medieval Times.

📝 Exam Link: The contractual spirit of feudalism → Magna Carta → modern constitutions. This is a key cause-and-effect chain!
Christendom

The Making of Christendom

Christianity grew from a persecuted faith to the official religion of Rome, then shaped all of medieval Europe.

Ancient Times

⛪ Development of Christianity in the Roman Empire

  • Christians were persecuted (迫害) by the Roman government for not worshipping Roman gods and emperors
  • Later, a Roman emperor stopped persecutions and granted religious freedom (宗教自由)
  • In AD 380, Christianity became the official religion (官方宗教) of the Roman Empire
  • The number of Christians increased dramatically
The Great Split

✝️ Split in Christianity

When the Roman Empire split into East and West, the Church also split:

  • Eastern Orthodox Church (東正教會) — in the East
  • Roman Catholic Church (羅馬天主教會) — in the West
Christendom

🌍 Formation of Christendom

Despite the split, Christianity continued to develop across Europe. With its spread, Christendom (基督教世界) emerged. The Roman Catholic Church's influence in Western Europe grew continuously.

The Church

Influence & Functions of the Church

The Church had a very high position among Europeans and influenced every aspect of life.

Power of the Pope

👑 The Pope (教皇) — Leader of the Church

  • Everyone, including the king, had to obey the Pope
  • The Pope could excommunicate (驅逐出教會) kings and nobles who disobeyed
  • The Church was wealthy — owned lots of lands, collected taxes, received possessions from nobles
Political

🏛️ Political Function

The king had to be crowned (加冕) by the Pope. The Pope settled disputes and mediated wars, helping maintain political stability in Europe.

Legal

⚖️ Legal Function

The Church made laws and set up courts (法庭). This helped maintain law and order.

Social

🤝 Social Services

The Church looked after the elderly and children, treated patients, and built roads and bridges.

Educational

📚 Church Schools

The Church set up many church schools teaching Latin and religious knowledge.

Cultural

📖 Preserving Classics

Monks (教士) in monasteries copied ancient texts and classics, preserving classical civilization.
